Author's Note...

This page has been a long time coming. I came up with the following metaphor for anxiety 1-2 years ago. I've shared it with a few people, and this year (2016) when #BellLetsTalk popped up on my twitter feed, I thought - why not share? I knew I couldn't get it written up that day, but I made it a goal to post something in the next few days.

Apparently my brain really liked the idea, as I woke up in the middle of the night with my mind going to town on how to write it. As I was thinking about the right words and cadence, another idea popped into my head - "This would be more fun and memorable as a comic." I went on a short vacation to New York City last weekend, and spent a good chunk of Monday morning outlining and drawing; I polished it off when I got home. (In case you're wondering: comics are. a lot. of work. But totally worth it!)

So here we are - my little blog post gone a little out of hand. As I hope is abundantly obvious, this is simply one way of looking at the experience of anxiety. It describes a little of my own experience, and one of the ways I've dealt with it. It may not be for everyone, but I hope it's useful to some - either in understanding their own anxiety, someone else's, and most importantly, treating yourself with kindness and love.

In some ways, this particular metaphor is outgrowing its usefuleness to me - it strikes me now as a little too pat. But that's just a sign of growth (I hope) and moving on to the next thing. Expect the next comic in a year or two! :)
