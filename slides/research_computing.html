Things to Consider

Cost

Cost is not just money.

Paying for hardware, software...but also time, experience, people's effort, sustainability, etc.

Figure out potential costs and choose best option available to you

How to do that:

  • Know the possibilities and limitations of your funding and institutional resources
  • Consider experience of yourself and colleagues
  • Consult with on-campus experts
  • This talk!

Scale

Scale impacts ALL later decisions

  • Data storage, hardware, software licensing
  • Compute method + parallelization

Over-estimate by at least an order of magnitude

  • If you run 100 simulations, you will almost certainly want to run 400-500, just to try.
  • You run your 100 simulations, then need to re-run them because of a software bug, then to test different conditions, then...
  • More computing => more data, more time, etc. etc.
